Kung Fu Movies

My top 3 movies of all

The Drunkin Master

"Drunken Master" (1978) is a kung fu
film starring Jackie Chan as Wong Fei-hung,
a young, rebellious martial artist who is
forced to train with his uncle, a gruff master
of the "Drunken Fist" style of kung fu.
This style involves incorporating the erratic
movements of a drunk person into fighting techniques.
Wong's initial disobedience and arrogance lead him
to be sent to his uncle's harsh training,
but he eventually learns to control and master
the drunken style, showcasing the power of discipline
and overcoming adversity.

Ipman Movie

The "Ip Man" movie series tells the story of Ip Man,
a Wing Chun grandmaster and Bruce Lee's teacher,
primarily set in the 1930s and 1940s. The films focus on
events in his life during the Sino-Japanese War,
including his time in Foshan and later in Hong Kong.

Kung Fu Hustle

Kung Fu Hustle" is a live-action cartoon-style martial arts
comedy film that blends kung fu action with slapstick humor
and a touch of Looney Tunes conventions. The film follows a
small-time gangster named Sing who accidentally discovers the
legendary kung fu masters and their village. While the film
features extreme violence, it's often presented in a lighthearted
and exaggerated way, drawing comparisons to classic cartoon styles.
